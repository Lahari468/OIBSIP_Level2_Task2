Marie Curie

(1867 - 1934)

The first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win in two different sciences

Pioneer of Radioactivity

Marie Curie's groundbreaking research on radioactivity revolutionized our understanding of physics and chemistry. Her discovery of radium and polonium opened new frontiers in science and medicine, paving the way for innovations like X-ray machines and cancer treatments.

"Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood. Now is the time to understand more, so that we may fear less."

What I admire most about Marie Curie is her relentless pursuit of knowledge despite facing immense challenges as a woman in science. Her dedication to research, even in poor working conditions, and her selfless work during World War I establishing mobile X-ray units demonstrate both scientific brilliance and profound humanity.

Key Moments

1867 - Born Maria Skłodowska in Warsaw, Poland
1891 - Moved to Paris to study at Sorbonne
1903 - Awarded Nobel Prize in Physics (shared with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel)
1911 - Awarded Nobel Prize in Chemistry
1934 - Died from aplastic anemia caused by prolonged exposure to radiation

"We must believe that we are gifted for something and that this thing must be attained."
